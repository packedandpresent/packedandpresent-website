Disney World is Great — But I Prefer Universal

April 29, 2026  ✦  Packed & Present Travel
Home → Blog → Disney World vs. Universal Orlando

There! I said it! But before you come with pitchforks, hear me out. Disney World is magical — nobody is disputing that. But if you are traveling with an adventurous child and you can only do one theme park vacation, Universal wins. And it's not particularly close.

Before the Disney adults come for me — hear me out. This isn't about which park is better. It's about which park is right for your family. And that answer depends entirely on who your kids are right now.

Disney Is Built for Everyone. Universal Is Built for Thrill Seekers.

Disney's greatest strength is also what makes it the wrong choice for certain families. It is designed to appeal to every age, every temperament, every attention span. That means a significant portion of the park is geared toward younger children — character meets, gentle rides, parades, and princess dining experiences that cost more than your car payment.

If your child is past the princess phase and asking for something with more adrenaline, Disney will leave them underwhelmed. Universal will not.

From Hagrid's Magical Creatures Motorbike Adventure to the Velocicoaster — one of the best roller coasters in the country — Universal delivers an intensity that Disney's flagship rides simply don't match. If your kid has ever watched a Harry Potter movie and dreamed of being inside that world, Universal is not optional. It's mandatory.

The Immersive Experience Is Unmatched

The Wizarding World of Harry Potter changed what theme parks could be. Walking into Hogsmeade for the first time — butterbeer in hand, Hogwarts looming in the background — is an experience that hits differently than even the best Disney land. Universal understood that today's park guest doesn't just want to ride something. They want to be inside the story.

Epic Universe, Universal's newest expansion, doubles down on this. The park is adding worlds based on franchises that are actively relevant to kids right now. Disney is still leaning heavily on legacy IP and nostalgia. There is absolutely a place for nostalgia — but nostalgia alone doesn't justify the price tag.

Diagon Alley at Universal — the immersive detail is extraordinary. That dragon breathes actual fire.

Let's Talk About the Price Tag

Disney World is expensive. Aggressively, almost defiantly expensive. Between park tickets, Lightning Lane, resort hotels, and dining, a family of four can easily spend $5,000 to $7,000 on a week-long trip — and still feel like they missed things.

Universal is not cheap either, but the value proposition is stronger. The parks are more compact, which means less walking and less time wasted. You can realistically experience both Universal Studios Florida and Islands of Adventure in two days. Disney requires a week and you'll still leave things undone.

Know your child, know what they want from the experience, and spend your money accordingly.

The character experiences at Disney are genuinely special — and worth every penny for the right age

So When Should You Choose Disney?

If your children are under 8, Disney is probably the right call. The character experiences, the gentler rides, the sheer wonder of seeing a young child meet their favorite princess for the first time — that is real and it is worth it. Disney at that age is incomparable.

But if your kids are older, if they want rides that make their stomachs drop, if they are Harry Potter fans or Jurassic World fans or Nintendo fans — take them to Universal. They will talk about it longer.

Honorable Mention: Legoland for Families with Littles on a Budget

If your kids are between 2 and 8 and you're watching the budget closely, Legoland Florida deserves serious consideration. It's specifically designed for that age group, significantly less expensive than both Disney and Universal, and kids in that window are absolutely obsessed with it. The rides are accessible, the theming is playful, and you won't spend the entire day managing a meltdown from a five-year-old who can't ride anything.

One honest caveat: if your kids are 9 or older, skip it. They will be bored. Legoland knows its audience — and its audience is little.

Legoland Florida — an underrated gem for families with younger kids and a smart budget

The Bottom Line

Disney World is a great park. Universal is the better vacation for the right family. Legoland is the smartest choice for families with young children who don't want to spend Disney money.

The mistake most families make is choosing based on brand loyalty rather than where their kids actually are right now. Your kids change fast. A park that would have been perfect at age 5 might completely miss at age 9 — and vice versa.

Your kids don't need the most expensive option. They need the right one.
